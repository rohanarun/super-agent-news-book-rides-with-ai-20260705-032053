How to evaluate and use this workflow

How to request your first AI-assisted ride comparison

  1. Define your intent clearly. Explain your origin, destination, preferred pickup time, and constraints like luggage. The more concrete you are, the easier it is for the agent to open the right pages and check relevant options without guesswork.
  2. Ask for explicit comparison. Instruct the agent to check multiple services side by side and summarize ETAs and fare ranges. This mirrors how a human assistant would work and avoids single-app bias.
  3. Review pickup details. Before proceeding, have the agent read back the pickup pin, building notes, and any driver instructions. This reduces common errors in busy urban environments.
  4. Leverage saved routes. For commutes or frequent trips, let Super recall prior routes from its computer-use cache so the setup time drops dramatically on future runs.
  5. Confirm before submission. Always require a pause for confirmation. Super is built to wait for approval, keeping you in control of any third-party action.

Implementation checklist

  • Verify that you are logged into the ride services you want compared before starting the agent workflow.
  • Store clear names for frequent locations so the agent can reuse them accurately later.
  • Decide in advance which fields require manual confirmation, such as surge pricing acceptance.
  • Limit the agent’s session scope to ride-booking tasks to reduce unintended actions.
  • Re-run the workflow at different times to understand price volatility patterns.
  • Periodically review cached routes to ensure addresses and preferences remain current.

Risks and limits

  • Dynamic pricing changes. Prices and ETAs can change between comparison and confirmation, so the final screen must always be checked by the user.
  • Security considerations. As reported in 2026 coverage of computer-use agents, broader access increases attack surfaces, making permission discipline essential.
  • Regional availability. Not all ride services offer equivalent web interfaces in every region, which can limit comparison depth.
  • Latency vs urgency. For urgent rides, the extra confirmation steps may feel slower than manual booking.
